# Factory Warehouse

- **Receiving** – Inspecting and accepting raw materials (chemicals, detergents, packaging materials, spare parts, etc.) from suppliers.
- **Storage** – Storing chemicals, detergents, softeners, etc. in designated areas according to relevant safety standards.
- **Inventory Management** – Tracking stock levels, setting reorder points, and planning ahead to prevent stock shortages.
- **Customer Item Handling** – Sorting, tagging, and tracking clothes/linen/fabric items received from customers for laundry.
- **Clean Linen/Garment Storage** – Storing washed products (hotel linen, uniforms, bedsheets, etc.) in a clean environment prior to delivery.
- **Packaging & Labeling** – Packaging items before delivery and labeling them by customer/order.
- **Order Picking & Sorting** – Picking the correct items for each customer order and sorting them for dispatch.
- **Dispatch & Logistics Coordination** – Coordinating with delivery vehicles to ensure timely delivery.
- **Quality Control Check** – Performing a final inspection of products leaving the warehouse for quality (cleanliness, damage, missing items).
- **Chemical & Hazardous Material Safety Management** – Ensuring compliance with Safety Data Sheets (SDS), fire safety, and ventilation control for chemicals such as detergents, bleach, and solvents.
- **Space Optimization** – Using shelving systems, rack organization, and FIFO/FEFO (First In First Out / First Expired First Out) systems.
- **Returns & Damaged Goods Handling** – Managing damaged items or items returned by customers.
- **Documentation & Record Keeping** – Maintaining delivery notes, invoices, stock reports, and audit trails.
- **Equipment & Supply Storage** – Storing machine spare parts, cleaning tools, and maintenance equipment.
- **Coordination with Production Floor** – Coordinating with the washing/drying/ironing departments to ensure smooth material flow.

# Hotel Warehouse

### 1. Par Level Management (Core Function)
Defines the amount of linen (e.g., bedsheets, towels) required per room for a hotel.

- **Capability:** Admin can review and, if needed, adjust the par level by linen type.
- **Purpose:** To prevent linen shortages while avoiding excessive overstocking.

### 2. Standing Order & Exchange Cart System
Manages linen items that need to be exchanged on a daily or periodic basis.

- **Capability:** A "one-for-one" exchange system where washed linen is swapped for used linen.
- **Purpose:** To forecast daily requirements and automatically supply linen without needing to place an order with the factory (Standard Exchange Cart Model).

### 3. Inventory Monitoring & Low Stock Alert
Provides real-time visibility into the current quantity of clean linen in the warehouse.

- **Capability:** View current stock quantities on a dashboard. The system triggers an alert when quantity falls below a set level.
- **Purpose:** To know in advance whether there is sufficient linen for hotel rooms.

### 4. Linen Lifecycle & Traceability (Client-side view)
Checks the condition of linen items present in the warehouse.

- **Capability:** Scan RFID/QR codes to check how many times an item has been washed and whether its condition is still good.
- **Purpose:** To monitor for lost or damaged items.

### 5. Reject/Damage Handling
Manages defective linen items found during warehouse inspection.

- **Capability:** Log damaged items, or items that were washed but are still not clean, as "Rejects."
- **Purpose:** To provide feedback to the factory and enable tracking for corrective action.
